# how I actually make the call
Building a product, not a hackathon demo
strategy
problemMost hackathon security tools stop at a model that works, treating accuracy as the only metric that matters.
the callWe scoped Vigilance Vision around two different customers, homeowners and local law enforcement, each with different needs and different willingness to pay, and built a tiered pricing model around that split instead of chasing accuracy alone.
outcomeWon 3rd place at KAVACH, judged as much on the business case as the model behind it.
Letting users override my assumptions
user research
problemWe designed the first version of a navigation device for visually impaired users around the signals we assumed mattered most: obstacles, distance, direction.
the callTalking to the people who'd actually wear it changed the priority list entirely. A hand reaching toward them, or a subtle shift nearby, mattered more than what we'd built for. We rebuilt around what they told us, not what we'd guessed.
outcomeReshaped the model's feature priorities and shipped a working prototype validated with NGOs and the people it was actually for.
The cheap fix wasn't the right fix
tradeoffs
problemA Spark cost-optimization project at BlackRock looked like a pure infrastructure question: cut cluster spend.
the callThe lowest-cost technical fix would've helped the infra bill and broken how 20+ downstream datasets were consumed. I chose partition and write-mode changes that took longer to design but didn't force any other team to change how they worked.
outcomeCut cluster utilization 30–45% without a single downstream team noticing the difference.
The win wasn't the win I planned for
ship & learn
problemWhen a pipeline failed, analysts lost hours manually tracing logs to find the root cause.
the callAt a company hackathon, we built a tool that turned raw failure logs into a plain-language summary instead of a wall of text, then watched how people actually used it after launch instead of assuming the job was done.
outcomeThe bigger value turned out to be onboarding: the tool got adopted across 4 teams to ramp up new hires, a use case we only found after shipping.
